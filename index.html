Designing Systems That Refuse to Die

April 2026 · 5 min read

In a world of unstable networks, unexpected crashes, and constant change, the real challenge is not building something — it's keeping it alive.

A “keep-alive mindset” means designing systems that expect failure. Instead of asking “how does it work?”, we ask: “what happens when it breaks?”

True resilience comes from redundancy, monitoring, and simplicity. The fewer moving parts, the harder it is to kill the system.
